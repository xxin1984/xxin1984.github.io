I am currently an associate professor in Beijing Institute of Technology (Director: Prof. Heyan Huang). Before, I obtained the bachelor degree and the master degree (Supervisor: Prof. Juanzi Li) from Tsinghua University (Beijing) in 2006 and 2008, respectively, and obtained the Ph. D degree (Supervisor: Prof. Irwin King and Prof. Michael R. Lyu) from the Chinese University of Hong Kong (Hong Kong, China) in 2011. I visited Microsoft Asia under the "Star Track" program (Collaboration Mentor: Chin-Yew Lin) in 2013. From the day I began to conduct research, my interests have been focused on probabilistic graphical models. Besides, in the era of LLM, I also guide my students on the topic of building knowledge graphs and encyclopedia entries, I hope these work can be applied in educational scenarios.

1. Probabilistic Graphical Models

  • Recursive Semi-Markov model
    Xin Xin, Jinlong Li, and Zeqi Tan. N-ary Constituent Tree Parsing with Recursive Semi-Markov Model. In Proceedings of the ACL 2021 (CCF A).
  • An unified model of semi-CRF and tree-CRF:
    Rui Wang, Xin Xin, Wei Chang, Kun Ming, Biao Li, and Xin Fan. Chinese NER with Height Limited Constituent Parsing. In Proceedings of the AAAI 2019 (CCF A). (Corresponding)
  • HMM with low-rank properties:

    Xin Xin, Irwin King, Hongbo Deng and Michael R. Lyu. A Social Recommendation Framework Based on Multi-scale Continuous Conditional Random Fields. In Proceedings of the CIKM 2009 (CCF B).
  • Constrained tree-structure CRF:
    Xin Xin, Juanzi Li, Jie Tang and Qiong Luo. Academic Conference Homepage Understanding Using Constrained Hierarchical Conditional Random Fields. In Proceedings of the CIKM 2008 (CCF B).

2. Knowlege Graph and Encyclopedia Entry Generation

  • Semantic Parsing for Texts, Audios, and Images: To understand the semantic structure of these social media content.
  • Automatic Knowledge Graph and Encyclopedia Entry Construction: To convert to Web data into structured knowledge graph data.
  • Fine-Grained Semantic Retrieval: To retrieval the information from the granularity of syntactic analysis.
  • Reliable Fact Inference: To conduct explainable LLM inference based on logic theories.
